About the Artist

Hello! Thanks for stopping by. I am Angela Lewis, a self-taught artist in Utah creating mixed media pieces. My formal education is in psychology and elementary education with training in applied behavioral analysis.

My passion for art started with my Grams, Grandma Ramona: not just during arts and craft times, but through Ramona's love for sewing her own clothing. Gram's clothes were always carefully crafted with curated, surface pattern designed fabrics. She adored polka dots, florals, and repeat patterns bordering solid prints. Soon, my own admiration of those fabrics spread to admiration of graffiti art in the neighborhoods around me, the architural structures on family vacations, and classic art pieces at museums. My curiosity and cleverness has led to experiences with a multitude of art forms. I delight in acrylic painting, watercolor, colored pencil art, lino cuts, mixed media, and digital art. Hours of YouTube tutorials, reading through books, and honest attempts have helped hone my skills to date.

I am passionate about and inspired by:

  • the cultures of Mexico, Texas, and Louisiana.
  • vibrant colors and color therapy.
  • women empowerment.
  • floral and fauna.
  • nostalgic movie characters and scenes from the '80s and '90s.
  • fantasy.

Each of my pieces tell a story bringing awareness, reviving a memory, or being playful.

When I'm not creating or collecting inspirational photos, I am enjoying life with my two boys. I love baking, cooking, nature walks, watching movies (cult classics and horror are among the favorites), listening to music, and playing video games.
