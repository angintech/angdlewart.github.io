About the Artist

Angela Lewis is a self taught artist in Utah creating mixed media pieces. Her formal education is in psychology, elementary education, with training in applied behavioral analysis.

Her passion for art started with her Grandma Ramona: not just during arts and craft times, but through Ramona's love for sewing her own clothing. Ramona's clothes were always carefully crafted with curated, surface pattern designed fabrics. Ramona adored polka dots, florals, or repeat patterns bordering solid prints. Soon, Angela's admiration of those fabrics spread to admiration of graffiti art in the neighborhoods around her, the architural structures on family vacations, and classic art pieces at museums. Angela's curiosity and cleverness led to experiences with a multitude of art forms. She delights in acrylic painting, watercolor, colored pencil art, lino cuts, and digital art for mixed media art pieces. Hours of youtube tutorials and reading through books has helped her hone skills to date.

She has passion for and is inspired by:

  • the cultures of Mexico, Texas, and Louisiana.
  • vibrant colors and color therapy.
  • women empowerment.
  • floral and fauna.
  • nostaligic movie characters and scenes from the '80s and '90s.
  • fantasy.

Each of her pieces tell a story bringing awareness, reviving a memory, or being playful.

When she is not creating or collecting inspiration photos, she is enjoying life with her two boys. She takes pleasure in baking, cooking, nature walks, and watching movies (cult classics and horror are among the favorites).
